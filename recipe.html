Gulab Jamun

Ingredients:
For Jamun Balls: 1 cup of Khoya (reduced milk solids), 1/4 cup of all-purpose flour (maida), 1/4 cup of fine semolina (sooji), A pinch of baking soda, 2 tablespoons of ghee (clarified butter), 2-3 tablespoons of milk (as needed to form a soft dough)
For Sugar Syrup:1 cup of sugar, 1/2 cup of water, 1/2 teaspoon of cardamom powder, 1 teaspoon of rose water or kewra water (optional), A few saffron strands (optional)

Recipe:
1. Prepare the Sugar Syrup: In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Bring it to a boil, stirring to dissolve the sugar. Add cardamom powder and saffron strands. Simmer the syrup for about 5-7 minutes until it reaches a slightly sticky consistency. Remove from heat and add rose water or kewra water. Set aside.
2. Prepare the Gulab Jamun Dough: In a mixing bowl, crumble the khoya. Add all-purpose flour, semolina, baking soda, and ghee. Mix well, and then gradually add milk to form a soft, smooth dough. Be careful not to over-knead.
3. Shape the Gulab Jamun Balls: Divide the dough into small portions and roll them into smooth balls. Ensure there are no cracks. The size of the balls can vary, but they should be small, as they will expand when frying.
4. Heat Ghee/Oil for Frying: Heat ghee or oil in a deep pan over medium heat. The temperature should be around 350°F (175°C).
5. Fry the Gulab Jamun: Gently slide the balls into the hot ghee or oil, a few at a time, and fry them on low to medium heat until they turn golden brown. Stir gently for even frying. Once fried, remove them using a slotted spoon and place them on absorbent paper to drain excess oil.
6. Soak in Sugar Syrup: While the Gulab Jamun balls are still warm, immerse them in the prepared sugar syrup. Let them soak for a few hours or overnight, allowing the syrup to penetrate the balls.
7. Garnish and Serve: Garnish with chopped pistachios or almonds if desired. Serve Gulab Jamun warm or at room temperature. Enjoy your homemade Gulab Jamun!
